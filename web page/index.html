What to be aware of in 2023 on the Internet

Special Offer: Free WHATEVER!

Excited about winning? Be cautious! Prize scams are common, with people losing millions. Scammers claim you won, but it's a lie. They ask for money or personal info.

Legitimate contests exist, but did you enter? If not, you didn't win. Here's how to avoid scams:


  • - Don't pay for prizes. Legit prizes are free. Scammers ask for fees—walk away.

  • - Never give financial info. No need for bank or credit card details—it's a scam.

  • - Avoid sharing personal info. Scammers use links to steal data—delete without clicking.

Do EASY work and get EASY money

Be vigilant against deceptive ploys enticing you with the allure of effortless jobs. These elaborate schemes, camouflaged as golden opportunities, often lead to financial pitfalls.

Beware of false claims proclaiming you as a triumphant victor, coercing you into parting with your money or divulging personal information.

Legitimate ventures uphold transparency and do not impose financial burdens – any such demand is a clear indicator of fraudulent intent.

Safeguard your interests by steering clear of requests for financial and personal details, fortifying your defenses against the web of deceit.

Black Friday and Cyber Monday are two of the biggest shopping days of the year—with good reason. Retailers offer amazing deals on everything from electronics to clothing to home goods. But with so many bargains to choose from, it’s easy to fall for fake shopping websites.

Fake shopping websites are designed to look like real websites, but they’re actually scams. These websites will often offer incredibly low prices on popular items, but they’ll never actually deliver the products you order. In some cases, fake shopping websites aim to steal your credit card information.

You found your LOVE! or not?

Honey traps are deceptive schemes designed to manipulate individuals through romantic or sexual enticement for ulterior motives. In these scenarios, perpetrators use charm and allure to establish trust and intimacy with their targets.

The intention is often to extract sensitive information, compromise security, or advance a hidden agenda. Honey traps are prevalent in espionage, where agents may exploit personal relationships to gain access to confidential information.

Awareness and caution are crucial in identifying and avoiding falling victim to such deceptive tactics.

Elder Theft and Scams are at an All-Time High

According to the FBI’s latest Internet Crime Report, Americans over the age of 60 lost $1.7 billion to fraud last year. That’s the highest loss amount reported out of any age group.

Older adults are less likely to be tech-savvy and more likely to be home during the day to answer phone calls or reply to emails. They may have accrued a lifetime of savings, their own home, and a promising credit score.

Whether it’s by impersonating government officials or claiming to offer financial assistance, scammers are unrelenting in their attempt to con seniors.

But elder fraud isn’t only a hazard for the older population. Family members may lose property or assets if they were co-signed on a fraudulent loan with the elder victim. This can also lead to credit scores plummeting for anyone involved.
